> Written so anyone helping with outreach uses the same methodology.

## Why outreach is structured

Random outreach produces low-quality results — burned credibility with potential allies, exhaustion of the founder, and adversaries learning about the project before allies do. A structured outreach approach delivers:

- **Higher response rates** (warm intros vs. cold emails: ~70% vs ~5%).
- **Better-fit candidates** (vetting before contact, not after).
- **Compromise resistance** — the project's existence and details reach trusted ears before hostile ones.
- **Track record** — every outreach interaction is documented, so the Initiative can learn what works.

## Target archetypes

We have six categories of people we need. Each has a different ask, different compensation, different vetting.

### 1. Scholars (`'ulama`)

**Why we need them:** Pillar 1 (Knowledge) and Pillar 11 (Spiritual). Without scholars, Basira is a generic AI making up Islamic answers. With scholars, it is the trusted research tool 1.8B Muslims need.

**Specifically wanted:**

- **Trained credentials:** Al-Azhar / Qom / Deoband / Nadwa / Madinah / equivalent. Not random YouTube imams.
- **Active scholarly practice:** teaching, publishing, issuing fatawa, or institutional roles.
- **Cross-tradition representation:**
  - 4 Sunni madhahib (Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, Hanbali)
  - 2 Shia schools (Ja'fari, Zaydi/Ismaili)
  - Ibadi
  - Sufi traditions
- **Cross-country distribution:**
  - Pakistan / India / Bangladesh (Hanafi center of gravity)
  - Egypt / Levant (Al-Azhar; multiple madhahib)
  - Saudi Arabia / Gulf (Hanbali / Salafi traditions)
  - Iraq / Iran / Lebanon (Shia)
  - Morocco / Mauritania / West Africa (Maliki)
  - Indonesia / Malaysia (Shafi'i + cultural Islam)
  - Turkey (Hanafi; Ottoman scholarly heritage)
  - Oman (Ibadi)
  - Diaspora (US, UK, Canada — converts and second-generation contexts)

**What we offer:**

- **Paid retainer** ($500-2,000/month equivalent depending on engagement intensity, regional adjustment).
- **Per-review compensation** for content verification.
- **Pseudonymous Council membership** (after vetting; OPSEC required).
- **Network** with other scholars who would otherwise not collaborate cross-tradition.
- **Citations** — their verified work is part of permanent infrastructure.

**What we ask:**

- **First 30 days:** review 1 sample workflow (e.g., one fatwa we're considering ingesting). Honest feedback. No commitment beyond that.
- **First 90 days:** if comfortable, retain at modest hours (5-10 hrs/month).
- **First 12 months:** if mutual fit, possible Council nomination.

**Vetting questions** (we ask):

- What is your formal training, where, and when? Independently verifiable?
- Are you currently affiliated with any government / military / intelligence service?
- Have you ever publicly endorsed `takfir` against a major Sunni or Shia tradition?
- Do you have current commercial / consulting relationships that could conflict?
- Are you willing to operate pseudonymously in a multi-school context?

**Vetting questions** (we expect from them):

- Who is funding this? Any strings?
- How will my contribution actually be used?
- What is the dispute resolution if I disagree with content?
- Why should I trust this won't fork into a sectarian project?
- Can you pay me as promised?

### 2. Technologists

**Why we need them:** Pillars 1, 4, 5, 6, 12. Code doesn't write itself. Without senior engineers and ML researchers, Basira / Shahid / Hisn / Mizan stay documentation.

**Specifically wanted:**

- **Senior software engineers** with 7+ years experience in Node.js / TypeScript / Python.
- **ML / AI researchers** with applied experience in LLM fine-tuning, RAG, evaluation.
- **Arabic NLP specialists** (rare — high priority).
- **Security engineers** with privacy / cryptographic / threat-modeling experience.
- **Infrastructure engineers** (Postgres, Linux, Hetzner-type self-hosted experience).

**What we offer:**

- **Paid roles** (full-time or contract) at fair market rates per region — see `FINANCIAL_MODEL.md`.
- **MIT-licensed work** that contributors retain rights to.
- **No equity dilution games** — we are not a startup with an exit.
- **Mission-aligned work** that's resume-defining.
- **Public attribution** in commit history and release notes.

**What we ask:**

- **First 30 days:** small, well-scoped task or two. Pair on architecture decisions.
- **First 90 days:** if mutual fit, paid contract for a major epic.
- **First 12 months:** potential full-time role.

**Vetting questions:**

- Code samples or open-source contributions?
- Any prior work with mission-driven orgs?
- Comfort operating with security-sensitive code?
- For senior roles: comfort being a maintainer (review responsibility, not just contribution)?

### 3. Translators

**Why we need them:** Reach beyond English-speaking diaspora. Quality matters — bad translation undermines trust.

**Specifically wanted:**

- **Native speakers** of: Arabic, Urdu, Bahasa Indonesia, Bahasa Malaysia, Bengali, Turkish, Persian, Hausa, Swahili.
- **Bilingual professionals** familiar with technical and scholarly registers (not just casual conversation).
- **Some Islamic-content sensitivity** (knowing that "shaheed" in the Quran has different connotation than in news headlines).

**What we offer:**

- **Per-word or per-project compensation** at fair rates.
- **Named credit** in translated content.
- **Stable work** — translation is ongoing, not one-shot.

**What we ask:**

- Sample translation (200 words from existing repo content).
- Regional sensitivity check (specific local idioms vs. modern standard).

## Methodology — how we contact

### Warm intros are 14× better than cold

Numbers are rough but consistent across professional outreach research:

- **Cold outreach** to scholars: 3-7% response rate.
- **Warm intro** through trusted third party: 60-80% response rate.

Therefore: **prefer warm intros.** When warm path doesn't exist, we accept cold reach but bake that lower yield into our pipeline math.

### Building warm-intro paths

**Founder maps existing relationships:**

1. List people the founder personally knows / has met.
2. For each, ask: "Who do they know that fits one of the archetypes above?"
3. For top candidates without warm path: identify mutual connections via LinkedIn / Twitter / public scholarly networks.
4. Ask the mutual to make the intro — short, factual, no overselling.

### Cold outreach (when no warm path)

- Personalized — never templated mass-blast.
- Reference their specific work (a paper, a fatwa, a public stance).
- Brief: 200 words max in first email.
- Specific ask: "Would you read a 2-page brief?" Not "join the project."
- One follow-up after 2 weeks. No more after that without new context.

## Pipeline tracking

Targets move through stages. We track in `private/OUTREACH_TARGETS.md`:

```
Stage 1: Identified         (added to list; not yet contacted)
Stage 2: Warm-intro pending (awaiting third-party introduction)
Stage 3: First contact      (initial message sent)
Stage 4: Engaged            (responded, dialogue in progress)
Stage 5: Vetting            (mutual evaluation)
Stage 6: Active             (working with us in some capacity)
Stage 7: Declined / inactive (clearly out, archived)
```

For each target: name (or pseudonym), archetype, country/sect, stage, last-contact date, next action, notes.

## Cadence and capacity

**Founder bandwidth assumption:** 5-10 outreach interactions per week sustainably.

**Therefore:** identify 30-50 priority targets in first month; first-contact 10-15 of them; pace remaining as bandwidth allows.

**Trying to talk to everyone at once = nobody well.**

## Vetting before invitation to Council

Per `COMMITTEE.md`, vetting for Council membership is rigorous: sponsorship, independent credential verification, OPSEC briefing, conflict-of-interest disclosure, cooling period, supermajority vote. Most outreach contacts will NOT become Council members — they will be:

- Paid contributors (Tier 1 in `FINANCIAL_MODEL.md`)
- Bounty / grant recipients (Tier 2)
- Open-source contributors (Tier 3)
- Institutional partners (Tier 4)
- Advisors (non-voting)

The Council is small (12). Most contributions happen outside it. This is appropriate.

---

## Adversary considerations

Outreach itself has adversary surface:

- **Adversary observes outreach pattern** → learns about the project before it's defended.
- **Adversary infiltrates as a candidate** → vetting catches most; ongoing OPSEC catches more.
- **Adversary impersonates the founder** to candidates → mitigated by signed messages, repo-based verification.
- **Adversary discredits reached candidates** publicly → most outreach is private; reached candidates are not publicly listed.

OPSEC for outreach:
- Use the project's verified email (not personal).
- Sign emails with the project's PGP key (when established).
- Don't disclose the names of other reached candidates (privacy + adversary defense).
- Track which contacts asked for confidentiality and respect it.

## What success looks like (12-month outreach goal)

- **30-50 contacts initiated.**
- **15-25 substantive responses.**
- **5-10 active engagements (paid contracts, scholarly retainers, partner MoUs, advisory roles).**
- **3-5 candidates progressed toward Council membership.**
- **2-4 institutional partnerships formalized.**
- **1-3 funding sources committed (grants, waqfs).**

Numbers are realistic, not aspirational. Outreach is multi-year work.

# The Manifesto

## A Statement of Mission, Vision, and Intent

---

## I. The Condition We Find Ourselves In

In the year 2026 of the Common Era — 1447 of the Hijri calendar — the Muslim Ummah of 1.8 billion souls faces a new kind of challenge. It is neither purely military nor purely economic. It is **infrastructural**.

The systems that increasingly decide who is seen and who is silenced, who is targeted and who is spared, whose voice reaches the world and whose is buried in algorithmic obscurity — these systems are built, owned, and operated by a small number of corporations concentrated in a handful of nations. The Ummah neither builds them, owns them, nor governs them. Yet the Ummah is among the most surveilled, the most censored, and the most targeted populations of these systems.

This is not a conspiracy. It is a structural fact, documented by journalists, researchers, and the corporations themselves. It did not arise from malice alone. It arose from the absence of Muslim participation in the construction of these systems.

**Absence creates vulnerability. Participation creates sovereignty.**

## II. What We Are Building

We are building an **ecosystem of open AI infrastructure and defensive tools** serving three purposes:

### Purpose 1: Preservation of Knowledge (`hifz al-ilm`)

Making the vast corpus of Islamic scholarly knowledge — the Quran, the Hadith sciences, the classical tafseer, the schools of fiqh, the works of the polymaths — accessible, searchable, and useful for every Muslim with an internet connection, in their native language, with scholarly integrity preserved.

### Purpose 2: Documentation and Witness (`shahadah`)

Building permanent, cryptographically-verified archives of AI-driven harms against civilians — whether in Gaza, Xinjiang, Kashmir, Myanmar, or anywhere a human being is targeted by an algorithm. The Quran commands Muslims to stand for justice "even against yourselves" (4:135). We build the tools to make witness possible.

### Purpose 3: Defense of the Defenseless (`himayat al-mustad'afin`)

Tools that defend innocent people from algorithmic oppression: counter-surveillance infrastructure, deepfake detection, privacy-preserving communications, and accountability mechanisms for platforms that censor, target, or exclude. These are not luxury goods. These are survival tools for millions.

## III. What We Are Not

We are not a political movement. We do not endorse parties, regimes, or factions.

We are not a sectarian project. We serve Muslims of every school of thought equally, and non-Muslims who share our values are equally welcome to contribute.

We are not nationalist. We do not prioritize the interests of any single country over the Ummah as a whole.

We are not anti-Western, anti-American, anti-Israeli, anti-Chinese, or anti-any-people. We are against the *use of technology to harm innocents*, regardless of who builds it or who wields it.

We are not seeking to build "Muslim ChatGPT" or to compete with American AI labs on their terms. That is the wrong game. We are building **different infrastructure for different purposes** — infrastructure the existing labs will never build because it is not in their commercial or political interest.

We are not a closed club. Every Muslim is already a member by virtue of being Muslim. Every non-Muslim of goodwill is welcomed as an ally. There are no membership fees, no loyalty tests, no ideological purity requirements beyond the few non-negotiable principles stated in `PRINCIPLES.md`.

## IV. Our Theory of Change

We reject three common theories of change and adopt a fourth.

**Rejected: Revolution.** The belief that a single dramatic action will change the trajectory of history. Too costly, too unlikely, too prone to capture.

**Rejected: Appeal.** The belief that if we simply explain the harm clearly enough, those causing it will stop. They will not. They have been told. They know.

**Rejected: Withdrawal.** The belief that Muslims should disengage from modern technology and return to some idealized past. This is surrender. It has been tried (Ottoman printing press ban, 1485–1729) and it produced three centuries of decline.

**Adopted: Construction.** The belief that lasting change comes from building alternatives so good, so open, so trusted that they reshape the landscape by their existence. This is how Bayt al-Hikmah operated. This is how Al-Azhar was founded. This is how Ibn Sina's Canon of Medicine displaced inferior texts for 500 years across three civilizations.

**We build. We publish. We share. We build again.**

## V. The Time Horizon

This is a **multi-generational project**.

Year 1 is for principles, architecture, and initial tools.
Year 3 is for commercial viability in at least one vertical (likely Islamic research tooling).
Year 5 is for ecosystem emergence — other builders joining, forking, extending.
Year 10 is for defensive infrastructure at scale.
Year 20 is for genuine Muslim digital sovereignty.

No one should expect immediate triumph. Salah ad-Din did not recover Jerusalem in a year. He built for twenty years, patiently, before the opportunity came. This is the model.

We are not in a hurry. We are in a direction.

## VI. What This Requires of Contributors

**Sincerity (`ikhlas`)**: Do this for the sake of benefit to the Ummah and to humanity, not for personal fame, wealth, or power. Personal livelihood is legitimate; personal aggrandizement is not the goal.

**Excellence (`ihsan`)**: Build as if Allah is watching, because He is. Poor quality work is not acceptable. Shortcuts that compromise principles are not acceptable. Do the work well.

**Patience (`sabr`)**: This will be slow. Contributions will feel invisible for years. The rewards may come only to those who come after us. This is acceptable. This is how civilizations are built.

**Transparency (`wuduh`)**: If you disagree with a decision, say so publicly. If you see a problem, raise it in the open. If you make a mistake, acknowledge it. There is no shame in error, only in concealment.

**Unity (`wahdah`)**: Work alongside Muslims whose theology, practice, or background differs from yours. Do not attempt to convert fellow contributors to your school. Do not disparage theirs. The Prophet, peace be upon him, prayed with his companions of every background. We follow that example.

## VII. A Note to Future Generations

If you are reading this in 2036, 2046, or beyond — know that this began in 2026 with a small number of people who had no special authority, no great wealth, no institutional backing, and no certainty of success. It began with a conversation between a developer in Pakistan and an AI system, asking whether such a project was worth attempting.

We did not know how it would unfold. We did not know if it would survive our own generation. We knew only that the alternative — continued absence, continued dependence, continued vulnerability — was unacceptable.

If the project has grown beyond what we imagined, remember that scale without principles is corruption. Return to these founding documents. Hold the institution accountable to them.

If the project has faltered, know that the attempt was itself meaningful, and the next generation can try again with what we have left behind.

If the project has been captured by sectarians, nationalists, or commercial interests that violate its founding principles — **reject the capture**. Fork it. Rebuild it. The principles matter more than any individual institution.

## VIII. Closing

We begin in the name of Allah, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate.

We commit our intention to Him.

We ask for His guidance in what we attempt.

We ask for forgiveness for our shortcomings before, during, and after.

We invite every Muslim, and every person of goodwill, to join us — not in loyalty to any leader, but in loyalty to the principles written in these pages.

Read them. Challenge them. Improve them. Build upon them.

Then get to work.

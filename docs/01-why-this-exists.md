# Why This Exists — The Threat Model

## What AI Is Doing to Innocent People in 2026

This document exists so that no one — not a future contributor, not a skeptic, not an AI system reading these files to help build the project — can ask *"is this really necessary?"* without confronting the documented reality.

Everything below is sourced from public investigations by journalists, human rights organizations, academic researchers, and in several cases the perpetrating institutions themselves. Citations point to primary sources. Where sources are paywalled or may disappear, the reader is encouraged to verify through archival services.

This is not exhaustive. It is representative. The full record is larger and grows weekly.

## 1. Algorithmic Targeting of Civilians in Gaza

**The Lavender system.**
In April 2024, `+972 Magazine` and `Local Call` published an investigation based on interviews with six Israeli intelligence officers, who described an AI system called "Lavender" used to identify targets for airstrikes in Gaza. According to the reporting:

- The system marked approximately 37,000 Palestinians as suspected militants.
- Officers were given minimal time to review each target — roughly 20 seconds — before authorizing strikes.
- The known error rate of the system was approximately 10 percent, meaning thousands of targets were acknowledged by operators to be misidentified.
- A parallel system called "Where's Daddy" tracked targets to their family homes, where strikes were then carried out — killing wives, children, and neighbors alongside the target.

Source: Yuval Abraham, *"'Lavender': The AI Machine Directing Israel's Bombing Spree in Gaza,"* +972 Magazine, April 3, 2024.

**Why this matters to our mission:**
AI was not incidental to the civilian death toll. AI was the mechanism by which scale was achieved. When a human operator has 20 seconds per decision, the system makes the decision; the human is rubber-stamping output. This is the template for future conflict. The Ummah cannot afford to be a passive witness to the industrialization of this approach.

## 2. AI-Enabled Surveillance of Uyghurs in Xinjiang

The People's Republic of China has constructed the largest contemporary surveillance state targeting a religious minority. AI is central to it.

**Facial recognition specifically tuned for Uyghur identification.**
IPVM and The New York Times reported in 2020 on Huawei patents and internal documents describing facial recognition systems with "Uyghur alerts" — explicit ethnic classification triggering flags to police. Similar systems from Megvii, SenseTime, and Hikvision have been documented. Source: Paul Mozur, *"One Month, 500,000 Face Scans: How China Is Using A.I. to Profile a Minority,"* The New York Times, April 14, 2019.

**Integrated Joint Operations Platform (IJOP).**
Human Rights Watch's 2019 reverse-engineering of the IJOP mobile application revealed an app used by police and officials to collect, aggregate, and act on data about Xinjiang residents — including religious practice, travel, and "suspicious" activity. Source: HRW, *"China's Algorithms of Repression,"* May 2019.

**Detention and forced labor.**
Multiple investigations — including the Uyghur Tribunal (UK, 2021) — have concluded that systematic detention, forced labor, and cultural destruction have affected over a million Uyghurs. AI-powered surveillance has been a foundational enabler.

**Why this matters to our mission:**
This is what happens to a Muslim population when a powerful state can deploy AI without accountability and without a Muslim-controlled alternative infrastructure to document, expose, and counter it. It is a warning about what becomes possible.

## 3. Algorithmic Suppression of Palestinian Voices Online

Major platforms have systematically over-moderated content related to Palestine, Arabic-language speech, and Islam.

**Business for Social Responsibility (BSR) independent audit of Meta, 2022.**
Commissioned by Meta itself in the aftermath of May 2021 Gaza coverage suppression, the audit concluded that Meta's actions "appear to have had an adverse human rights impact ... on the rights of Palestinian users to freedom of expression." Arabic content was over-moderated relative to Hebrew. The word "shaheed" (martyr) was automatically classified as praise of terrorism despite its religious meaning.

Source: BSR, *Human Rights Due Diligence of Meta's Impacts in Israel and Palestine in May 2021,* September 2022.

**7amleh — The Arab Center for the Advancement of Social Media** has documented thousands of cases of Palestinian content being removed, accounts being suspended, and hashtags being suppressed across Meta platforms, TikTok, and X. Their annual reports catalog the scale.

**Human Rights Watch, 2023:** *"Meta's Broken Promises: Systemic Censorship of Palestine Content."* Documented over 1,000 cases from October–November 2023.

**Why this matters to our mission:**
The platforms where 1.8 billion Muslims communicate are configured, whether by design or by accident, to disadvantage Muslim expression. This is not a temporary accident. It is a structural feature. An Ummah dependent on these platforms is an Ummah whose voice can be turned down at any moment.

## 4. AI-Generated Deepfakes Targeting Muslim Women, Scholars, and Activists

**Deepfake pornography.**
A 2023 report by Home Security Heroes estimated that 98 percent of deepfake videos online are non-consensual sexual content, and 99 percent of those target women. Muslim women — particularly visible activists, journalists, and political figures — have been disproportionately targeted as a silencing tactic.

Rana Ayyub (Indian journalist), Ghada Oueiss (Al Jazeera journalist), and many others have publicly documented being targeted with AI-generated sexual imagery designed to intimidate them out of their work.

**Fabricated "evidence" against Muslim individuals.**
Deepfakes have been used to fabricate videos of Muslim scholars, imams, and activists making statements they never made — sometimes to discredit them, sometimes to justify prosecution.

**Why this matters to our mission:**
Deepfakes do not require frontier AI labs to produce. Open-source tools are sufficient. What is missing is a trusted, accessible, Ummah-serving infrastructure for **verification** — so that a Muslim community confronted with a suspicious video of their scholar has somewhere to turn.

## 5. Predictive Policing and Watchlists

**Post-9/11 watchlists in Western countries.**
The U.S. Terrorist Screening Database, the U.K. Prevent program, and equivalents in France, Germany, and elsewhere have incorporated increasingly algorithmic decision-making. Studies have documented disproportionate inclusion of Muslims, often with no notification or appeal mechanism.

**School-level predictive systems.**
The U.K. Prevent program has referred tens of thousands of people to "deradicalization" services, disproportionately Muslim children, based in part on algorithmic and behavioral flagging.

**Why this matters to our mission:**
Muslims in Western countries live under a layer of automated suspicion that is largely invisible to them. Defensive tools, legal resources, and public accountability require an infrastructure the Ummah builds for itself.

## 6. Biometric Data Compromise — the Afghan Example

When the United States and NATO withdrew from Afghanistan in August 2021, biometric enrollment devices (HIIDE — Handheld Interagency Identity Detection Equipment) containing iris scans, fingerprints, and biographical data of tens of thousands of Afghans who had worked with coalition forces fell into the hands of the Taliban.

Source: Ken Klippenstein and Sara Sirota, *"The Taliban Have Seized U.S. Military Biometrics Devices,"* The Intercept, August 17, 2021.

**Why this matters to our mission:**
Data collected with one intent, under one regime, can become a targeting list under another. Data sovereignty and data minimization are not abstract concerns. They have cost lives.

## 7. The Language Gap

Arabic is spoken by approximately 400 million people. Urdu, over 200 million. Bengali, over 200 million. Malay and Bahasa Indonesia, over 300 million combined. Persian, over 100 million. Turkish, over 80 million. Hausa, Swahili, and others add hundreds of millions more.

Yet every major AI system:
- Performs substantially worse on Arabic than on English.
- Treats classical Arabic (the language of the Quran and of 1400 years of scholarship) as an afterthought.
- Treats Urdu, Bengali, and most African Muslim languages as low-priority.
- Produces output in these languages that ranges from awkward to actively misleading.

Evaluations by academic researchers (e.g. the Masakhane project for African languages, multiple benchmark studies for Arabic LLMs) consistently demonstrate this gap.

**Why this matters to our mission:**
A generation of Muslim children is being educated by AI tutors that serve them inferior knowledge in their native languages. Scholars cannot use AI tools for classical Arabic research in any serious way. The language gap compounds every other disadvantage. It is not solved by waiting for the major AI labs to prioritize us; they have not and will not.

## 8. The Infrastructure Gap

Muslim-majority nations collectively operate near-zero percent of:
- Frontier model training infrastructure.
- Major AI labs.
- Large-scale compute (GPU clusters).
- Significant cloud infrastructure (the three hyperscalers — AWS, Azure, GCP — are all American; Alibaba Cloud is the main non-Western alternative).
- Foundational AI research published in top venues.

Exceptions exist — UAE's investments (G42, Technology Innovation Institute, Falcon LLM, Jais), Saudi Arabia's NEOM ambitions, Malaysia's Jomo Cloud, Indonesia's emerging ecosystem, Pakistani and Egyptian academic work — but the overall picture is severe under-capacity.

**Why this matters to our mission:**
Sovereignty requires infrastructure. Building software without infrastructure is building on rented land. Over the long term, the Ummah requires its own compute, its own data centers, its own research labs. This project alone will not solve that — but it can contribute a layer (application, data, trust) that complements emerging infrastructure work.

## The Pattern

The above is not a collection of unrelated grievances. It is a pattern:

1. **Technology is deployed** — sometimes by states, sometimes by corporations, sometimes by both.
2. **The burden falls disproportionately on Muslims** — in war zones, in diaspora, in authoritarian states, in democracies.
3. **Recourse is minimal** — because the Ummah does not own the infrastructure, set the standards, or control the platforms.
4. **Harm compounds** — each generation of tools builds on the previous.

We are not the first generation to notice. We may not be the last to fail to respond adequately. But we are the generation for which the cost of continued absence is now clearly visible.

## What This Document Is Not

This is not an argument for withdrawal from technology. It is an argument for **participation on our own terms**.

This is not an argument for Muslim-only tools that exclude others. It is an argument for Muslim-built tools that serve the vulnerable — which includes non-Muslims harmed by the same systems.

This is not an anti-American, anti-Chinese, or anti-Israeli document. It is an anti-atrocity document. When the offending state changes, our objection to the atrocity does not.

This is not a call to match frontier AI labs capability-for-capability. It is a call to build *different* capability — in the places those labs will not build, for the people they will not serve.

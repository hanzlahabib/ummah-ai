# Co-Founder Search

## How to find the right technical co-founder for the Initiative

> Co-founder courtship, not recruitment. Slow, deliberate, with structural fairness.

---

## Status

**Phase:** Open. No co-founder yet. Founder operating solo.

**Position:** Acceptable. Solo founder is the default; a co-founder joins only if they meet a high bar. **Wrong co-founder is worse than solo.**

---

## Why this document

Co-founder selection is the highest-leverage decision a solo founder makes. Wrong choice loses 2-3 years and burns the working relationship. Right choice 5-10×s the work.

This document specifies:

- The profile we are looking for.
- What we offer (which is structurally unusual).
- Where to find candidates.
- How to evaluate.
- How to onboard.
- How to disengage if it's not working.

It also documents what we are **not** doing — so that desperation doesn't lead to compromise.

---

## What we are looking for

A **technical co-founder** with:

| Requirement | Why |
|---|---|
| **10+ years of senior engineering / ML / infrastructure experience** | Can lead architecture decisions independently; doesn't need hand-holding |
| **Practicing Muslim** | Mission alignment must be religious, not just intellectual |
| **Already financially stable** | $500K capped return is not motivating to someone who needs an exit |
| **Has shipped real products** | Track record of actually getting things done, not just designing them |
| **Documented "durd" (visceral concern) for AI atrocities / Muslim tech sovereignty** | Mission alignment must be felt, not just spoken |
| **Multi-year commitment willingness** | Civilizational horizon, not startup quarterlies |
| **Cross-school comfort (Sunni / Shia / etc.)** | Will work on multi-tradition product daily |
| **Acceptance of capped equity + not-for-sale structure** | Financial model is a screen — anyone uncomfortable with it self-selects out |
| **Operational security awareness, or willingness to learn** | High-stakes infrastructure; threat model is real |

### Hard disqualifiers (no exceptions)

- Cannot accept the financial model (asks for traditional equity / exit).
- Cannot accept multi-school representation (insists on single sect).
- Cannot accept Council governance (wants founder-king setup).
- Public history of takfir, sectarianism, or extremism (left or right).
- Currently employed by frontier AI labs (irreconcilable conflict during tenure; departure required first).
- Currently consulting for any government's intelligence / military / interior services.

### Soft disqualifiers (require careful judgment)

- Has not built anything that lasted (could be situational; could be pattern).
- Asks "what's the exit" early in conversation (signals extraction mindset).
- Public political activism on partisan issues (could compromise Initiative neutrality).
- Recent burnout / personal crisis (timing matters; founder commitment is heavy).

## What we offer

Per `FINANCIAL_MODEL.md` and `COMMITTEE.md`:

### Financial

- **Same lifetime cap structure as founder:** tentatively $500,000 USD indexed. **Separate cap, not shared** — co-founder has their own $500K capacity.
- **Fair-market salary** during active tenure, within published bands.
- **Vesting-equivalent for cap:** the $500K accrues over 4 years. Leaving before vest = smaller realized return. Discourages flake.
- **No traditional equity.** No share of an exit (because there is no exit — entity is not for sale).
- **No favoritism over team:** salary multiple cap (5×) applies; co-founder doesn't earn 50× the lowest paid.

### Governance

- **Council seat** by virtue of the technical co-founder role.
- **Equal authority on technical decisions** — architecture, tech-stack, technical hiring.
- **Founder retains principle-level authority** during Phase G0 (foundation) and shares with co-founder + Council from Phase G1 onward.
- **Veto on principle-violating decisions** — co-founder can block any move that contradicts foundation documents, just as founder can.

### Conditions

- **4-year commitment expected** with 1-year reflection clause: at the 12-month mark, both sides can exit cleanly without prejudice. Salary continues to month 12 in either case.
- **Trial period (90 days)** before formal co-founder role is announced. During trial: senior contractor with significant scope.

## Profile-specific reality

### What this person likely looks like

A Pakistani / Egyptian / Malaysian / Indonesian / Turkish / Arab Muslim man or woman in their early 30s to early 40s. Senior engineer at a tech company (FAANG / frontier lab / major fintech / unicorn) — possibly already a tech lead or staff engineer. Has shipped products that millions use. Has somewhere between $100K and $1M in personal savings. Family or marriage stable enough to support a multi-year mission engagement. Active in their religious practice. Has tried existing Islamic AI tools and been visibly frustrated by their quality. Has thought about Muslim tech sovereignty before encountering this Initiative.

### What this person is NOT

- A junior engineer looking for their first big break.
- A consultant looking to bill hours.
- A startup-flipper between exits.
- A media-savvy "thought leader" without shipping history.
- A scholar who codes a little (scholars belong on the scholarly track, not the technical co-founder track).
- A non-Muslim tech ally (welcomed in many roles, but not the technical co-founder seat — the role's mission alignment requires religious conviction, and that requires being Muslim).

## Where to find them

### Public networks

- **FAANG / frontier-AI-lab Muslim engineers** — LinkedIn filter searches by nationality + experience + role.
- **Yaqeen Institute / Maydan Institute / Bayyinah Institute** technology contributors.
- **Quran.com / Salam.ai / Islamic open-source projects** — read GitHub contributor histories.
- **MuslimTech Conference / RIS Convention tech tracks** — past speaker rosters.
- **Pakistani / Egyptian / Indonesian senior tech alumni networks** of major universities (NUST, LUMS, FAST, GIK, AUC, Cairo University, ITB).
- **Muslim tech Twitter/X / Discord / Slack communities.**
- **Returnees** to Muslim-majority countries from Silicon Valley / Dubai / London — often hungry for meaning.

### Private signals

- People who **publicly criticize** the state of AI for Islamic content (with technical specificity, not just complaining).
- People who have **built** scholarly Islamic tools as side projects on GitHub.
- People who **left** AI labs over conscience reasons (reach out cautiously; some are sensitive about it).
- People who **donate** to mission-aligned organizations and have visible technical roles.

### Where NOT to look

- General tech recruiting platforms (Y Combinator, AngelList) — wrong selection bias.
- Cold-blast emails to "Muslim engineers in big tech" lists — extractive vibe.
- Within personal close-circle if relationship would be damaged by mismatch (family, marriage, current employer).

## Approach methodology

### Phase 1 — Soft contact (Month 1)

- Identify 10-15 candidates.
- Research each: read their public work, understand their stance, watch for signals of "durd."
- Cut to top 5-7.
- Reach out to top 5-7 with a **feedback request**, not a co-founder pitch:

```
Salam [Name],

I came across your work on [specific thing] and it resonates with
something I'm building. I'd value your honest feedback — 30 minutes,
no commitment.

Open-source initiative; full plan public:
https://github.com/hanzlahabib/ummah-ai

Brief: [link]

The architecture decisions (capped founder equity, pseudonymous
Council, multi-school content, not-for-sale clause) are unusual,
and I want to hear whether they hold up under technical scrutiny.

Worth a call in the next 2-3 weeks?
```

**Why feedback first:** Discovery, not pitch. Reduces pressure on both sides. Filters out extractive responders. Creates space for genuine engagement.

### Phase 2 — First conversations (Month 1-2)

For each candidate who responds positively: 1-hour conversation, ideally video.

Listen for 70% of the call. Topics that come up naturally:

- Technical depth on the architecture decisions.
- Visceral reaction to atrocity examples (Lavender, Xinjiang, Palestinian content suppression).
- Comfort with multi-school content and Council pseudonymity.
- Family / personal context that affects multi-year commitment capacity.
- Their existing thinking on Muslim tech sovereignty (or lack thereof).

After each call: 5-line summary in `private/OUTREACH_TARGETS.md` under co-founder tag. Categorize:

- **A — Pursue further.** Strong signals; want another conversation.
- **B — Maybe.** Interesting but unclear; revisit in a month.
- **C — Polite pass.** Not the fit. Maintain warm acquaintance.

### Phase 3 — Deeper engagement (Month 2-3)

For A-tier (typically 1-3 candidates):

- Multiple longer conversations (2+ hour calls; in-person if geography allows).
- Joint review of specific architecture decisions (does their thinking improve the design?).
- Introduce them to existing trusted contacts (mentors, advisors) for mutual reference.
- They meet relevant scholars (if any are engaged) to test multi-school comfort.
- Discuss family / financial / lifestyle implications of multi-year commitment.

### Phase 4 — Trial (Month 3-4 onward)

For 1 finalist:

- **90-day paid contract** as senior technical contributor.
- Specific scope (e.g., lead architecture review and produce v0.1 build plan).
- Day-to-day collaboration to test working chemistry.
- Both sides can walk away at end of 90 days with no awkwardness.

### Phase 5 — Formalization

After successful trial:

- Co-founder role formally announced.
- Compensation package finalized per `FINANCIAL_MODEL.md` (separate cap; vesting-equivalent).
- Council seat assigned.
- Public attribution in repo and announcement (or pseudonymous, per their preference).

---

## Trial-period evaluation criteria

By end of 90-day trial, both sides answer honestly:

### Founder evaluates co-founder

- Technical execution: did they ship what they said they'd ship?
- Mission alignment: did their decisions reflect the principles, even when no one was watching?
- Working chemistry: would I want to do this for 4 years?
- Conflict handling: when we disagreed, was the resolution fair and respectful?
- OPSEC: did they handle sensitive material with appropriate care?
- Scholar interaction (if any): did they treat religious work with appropriate humility?

### Co-founder evaluates founder + project

- Is the project actually what the docs claim?
- Is the founder operationally serious or performance-only?
- Are decisions made openly?
- Is the financial model real (paid as promised)?
- Is the work I'd be doing actually meaningful?
- Can I commit 4 years to this person and this mission?

If either side has serious doubts, the trial ends without bad blood. The 90-day contract is paid in full regardless of outcome.

## Disengagement (if it doesn't work)

If at any point — pre-trial, during trial, post-formalization — the relationship is not working:

### Pre-trial / during trial

- Either side ends conversations / contract with thanks.
- No public announcement (most outsiders don't know co-founder discussion is happening).
- Trial-period contract is paid in full.

### Post-formalization (if co-founder later doesn't work out)

- Documented in governance per `docs/06-governance.md`.
- Co-founder retains accrued cap value (vested portion).
- Council seat returns to vacancy / replacement process.
- Public statement is brief, factual, respectful.

The structure is designed so disengagement is possible without damaging either party. This is itself a feature — relationships thrive when exit is dignified.

---

## What if no one fits

It is **acceptable** to remain solo through Phase 0 (foundation) and even into Phase 1 (Basira MVP build) if no co-founder candidate clears the bar.

In that case:

- Solo founder continues with senior contractors filling specific gaps (security review, ML expertise, etc.).
- The Council, once formed, provides multiple voices that compensate for the missing co-founder voice.
- Co-founder search continues at lower intensity — patient and ongoing.

**Wrong co-founder = worse than solo.** This commitment is documented so that timeline pressure does not lead to bad decisions.

---

## Adversary considerations

Co-founder slot is a high-value target for capture attempts:

- **Plant** — adversary positions a candidate for the role. Mitigated by: long courtship process, multiple references, OPSEC vetting, principle-stress-tests during conversation.
- **Honey trap** — co-founder candidate uses personal closeness to compromise founder. Mitigated by: maintaining professional norms during courtship; financial / personal independence verified.
- **Information leakage** — pre-formal candidate learns details that later inform an attack. Mitigated by: most details are already public in the repo; no compartmentalized strategy is shared until formal role.

---

## Founder's commitment in this search

The founder commits to the following throughout:

- **No deception** — candidate sees the real plan, with all its uncertainty, not a polished pitch deck that overpromises.
- **No emotional manipulation** — appeals to mission are factual, not high-pressure.
- **Fair compensation always** — even unsuccessful trials are paid.
- **Documented respect** — even declined candidates are treated as colleagues; no dismissive responses, no public criticism.
- **Patience over urgency** — better to wait 12 months for the right person than to onboard the wrong person in 3 months.

---

## Closing

Building a multi-decade civilizational initiative is hard alone. With one trustworthy partner, it becomes possible. With the wrong partner, it becomes impossible.

This document is the discipline that improves the odds of finding the right partner without being trapped by the wrong one.

If the right person never emerges, the work continues alone — supported by the Council, the scholars, the contributors, and the institutional structures already documented. The Initiative's success does not depend on a co-founder existing. But it accelerates dramatically if one does.

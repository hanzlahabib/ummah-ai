# Code of Conduct

## The Rules of Engagement

This project brings together Muslims from every sect, school, and tradition, alongside non-Muslim allies. For it to function, we need shared rules of engagement. This document is those rules.

These are not suggestions. Violations are grounds for removal from the project.

---

## 1. Anti-Sectarianism is Enforced

### Who is welcome

Muslims across the full range of mainstream Islamic scholarly tradition — Sunni (Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, Hanbali; Ash'ari, Maturidi, Athari; Sufi, Salafi, traditionalist), Shia (Ja'fari/Twelver, Zaydi, Ismaili), Ibadi, and others within this tradition — are welcomed equally as contributors. Scope of "Muslim" for this purpose follows the consensus of mainstream Islamic scholarly institutions (see `docs/09-content-policy.md`). Non-Muslim allies who accept these principles are welcomed as collaborators and auditors.

### What is prohibited

- Declaring fellow contributors who operate within mainstream Islamic scholarly tradition outside of Islam (`takfir`).
- Using the project to promote one Muslim school over another.
- Disparaging the scholars, practices, or beliefs of any tradition within the project's scope.
- "Corrective" messaging aimed at converting fellow contributors to your school of thought.
- Attempting to exclude qualified contributors on sectarian or school-of-thought grounds.

### What is encouraged

- Representing multiple scholarly opinions when relevant, with sources.
- Explaining differences among Muslim schools factually, without judgment.
- Working alongside Muslims whose practice differs from yours with respect.
- Acknowledging that reasonable Muslim scholars disagree on many issues, and our project serves them all.

### In technical terms

When our tools produce Islamic content:
- Quranic content is common to all Muslims and presented without sectarian framing.
- Hadith collections span Sunni (Bukhari, Muslim, Tirmidhi, Abu Dawud, Nasa'i, Ibn Majah) and Shia (Al-Kafi, Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih, Tahdhib al-Ahkam, Al-Istibsar) sources. When a user queries, we cite from the traditions they select or from all traditions if unspecified, transparently.
- Fiqh content acknowledges the major schools (Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, Hanbali, Ja'fari, Zaydi, Ibadi) and any positions represented. We do not default to one.
- Tafseer, aqeedah, and other contested domains similarly represent plurality.

This is not relativism. It is honesty about the diversity of Muslim scholarship.

---

## 2. Transparency is Non-Optional

- If you disagree with a decision, disagree in public.
- Do not lobby privately for changes to the project. Open an issue.
- Do not form private factions or coalitions to pressure outcomes.
- If you have a conflict of interest (financial, political, institutional), disclose it.

The reasoning: backchannels are how projects get captured. If your position cannot be defended in public, it should not win.

---

## 3. Conduct Toward Contributors

### Required behavior

- Treat every contributor with the respect you would want for yourself.
- Assume good faith until evidence of bad faith is documented.
- Acknowledge the work of others. Do not claim credit for what you did not do.
- Correct your mistakes openly. Apologize when wrong.
- Teach when you can. Learn when you should.

### Prohibited behavior

- Personal attacks, insults, or harassment.
- Discrimination on the basis of race, gender, nationality, language, or any other protected category.
- Sexual harassment or inappropriate conduct.
- Doxxing (revealing private identity information of pseudonymous contributors).
- Sustained bad-faith engagement, trolling, or intentional disruption.
- Retaliation against anyone who raises concerns in good faith.

### A special note on women contributors

Muslim women have contributed to this civilization since its beginning. Aisha bint Abi Bakr (RA) taught the companions. Fatima al-Fihri founded the world's oldest continuously operating university (al-Qarawiyyin, 859 CE). Rabia al-Adawiyya was a foundational figure in Muslim spirituality.

Women contributors to this project are welcomed equally, addressed respectfully, and will not be subjected to gatekeeping or differential treatment. Anyone who harasses, excludes, or patronizes women contributors will be removed.

---

## 4. Technical Conduct

- Submit your own work. Attribute others'.
- Do not introduce malicious code, backdoors, or intentionally flawed logic.
- Do not commit secrets, credentials, or private data.
- Follow the project's contribution guidelines (`CONTRIBUTING.md`).
- When you break something, help fix it.

---

## 5. External Conduct

Contributors represent the project when they publicly identify as such. Behavior elsewhere that materially damages the project — for example, public harassment of other contributors on external platforms, or using project affiliation to legitimize harmful activity — is within scope of this code of conduct.

We do not police private lives. We do ask that public conduct under the project's banner reflect the principles.

---

## 6. Escalation and Enforcement

### For normal issues

1. Try to resolve directly with the person.
2. If that fails, raise the issue publicly in the project's designated channels.
3. If still unresolved, maintainers or governance bodies (once established) will mediate.

### For serious violations

For safety issues, harassment, or violations that require confidentiality to protect the reporter, use the private reporting channel once established. Reporters of good-faith concerns are protected from retaliation.

### Consequences

Depending on severity:
- A warning and required acknowledgment.
- Temporary suspension from contributing.
- Permanent removal from the project.
- In extreme cases (malicious code, targeted harassment, criminal acts), referral to appropriate authorities.

Decisions are documented publicly where doing so does not endanger a reporter.

---

## 7. A Note on Disagreement

Principled disagreement is welcome. It is how good decisions are made. You are free to:

- Argue that a technical decision is wrong.
- Argue that a strategic direction is mistaken.
- Argue that a principle in `PRINCIPLES.md` is being misapplied.
- Propose forks if your view does not prevail.

You are not free to:

- Sabotage the project because you lost an argument.
- Continue to re-litigate settled decisions indefinitely to exhaust other contributors.
- Mistake disagreement for oppression.

---

## 8. On AI Contributors

Increasingly, contributions to software projects involve AI assistants. This project accepts AI-assisted contributions under the following conditions:

- The human contributor is responsible for reviewing and approving every line of AI-generated content they submit.
- AI-generated content must comply with the principles in `PRINCIPLES.md` and the non-negotiables in `docs/07-non-negotiables.md`.
- AI assistants working on this project must be instructed to refuse deviations from the principles. See `docs/07-non-negotiables.md` for guidance addressed directly to AI systems.

---

## 9. Final Word

This code of conduct exists to protect the project, not to punish people. We prefer teaching to punishing. We prefer reconciliation to exile. But we will not tolerate behavior that poisons the commons we are trying to build.

If you are unsure whether something is acceptable, ask. It is better to clarify early than to cause harm.

*May Allah guide our conduct and grant us the wisdom to disagree well and the humility to recognize our own faults first.*

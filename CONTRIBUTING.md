# Contributing

## How to Join This Work

This project belongs to no one. It belongs to the Ummah. Anyone who accepts the `PRINCIPLES.md` and `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md` can contribute.

There are no gatekeepers. There are no membership fees. There is no application process. There is only work, done openly, evaluated on its merits.

---

## Before You Start

Read these, in order:

1. `README.md` — what this is
2. `MANIFESTO.md` — why it exists
3. `PRINCIPLES.md` — what is non-negotiable
4. `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md` — how we treat each other
5. `docs/03-open-vs-closed.md` — our disclosure framework
6. `docs/07-non-negotiables.md` — hard constraints

If you find yourself disagreeing with the principles, do not start contributing and hoping to change them from inside. Raise your disagreement first, in the open, and see whether it is accepted. Contributions that attempt to quietly undermine the principles will be reverted.

## Ways to Contribute

Contribution is not limited to code. All of the following are valuable:

### Research and documentation
- Adding historical precedents to `docs/02-historical-precedent.md`.
- Documenting AI-driven harms to the threat model in `docs/01-why-this-exists.md`.
- Translating documentation into Urdu, Arabic, Malay, Bahasa Indonesia, Bengali, Turkish, Persian, Hausa, Swahili, French, and other languages.
- Contributing to evaluation frameworks and benchmarks.

### Scholarly contribution
- Verifying Islamic content accuracy.
- Proposing representative sourcing across schools and traditions.
- Flagging theological errors or sectarian bias.

### Technical contribution
- Architecture proposals (once we move from foundation to architecture phase).
- Code contributions (once implementation begins).
- Security audits.
- Infrastructure hosting and operations.

### Strategic contribution
- Identifying partnerships, funding sources, distribution channels.
- Connecting the project to institutions (madaris, universities, NGOs, Islamic banks).
- Legal and governance advice.

### Defensive contribution
- Privacy and security engineering for activist users.
- Threat modeling for adversarial actors.
- Incident response if and when it is needed.

## What You Get — Honest Answer

A fair question every contributor should ask: *"If I contribute, what do I get?"* This project's financial structure is explicitly documented in [`FINANCIAL_MODEL.md`](FINANCIAL_MODEL.md). In summary:

### If you submit an open-source contribution (code, docs, translation, testing, review):

- **Full license to your own work.** Your contribution is licensed under MIT / CC BY-SA 4.0 — meaning **you can use it anywhere, forever, including in your own commercial products.** You are not signing over rights to us.
- **Public attribution** (or pseudonymous if you prefer). Credited in release notes, contributor lists, git history.
- **Right to fork** per Principle 8 — always.
- **Reference value** for your career or academic work.
- **Network access** to scholars, technologists, institutional partners.
- **Mission participation** — your commits are part of the permanent record of building Ummah digital infrastructure.

You do NOT automatically receive salary, equity, or revenue share from the unpaid contribution tier. But paid paths exist (see below).

### Paid paths for contributors

- **Full-time roles** — fair-market salary, published ranges, no equity (we are not a startup with an exit).
- **Scholar compensation** — paid per-review, retainers, or grants.
- **Feature bounties** — agreed-in-advance pay for significant external contributions.
- **Retroactive grants** — case-by-case awards for exceptional already-completed work.
- **Contracted specialists** — security audits, translations, legal, accounting.

Target: **5% of net revenue** flows through bounty / grant pools per `FINANCIAL_MODEL.md`.

### What the founder does NOT get

This matters to contributors, so it is stated openly:

- **Founder has a lifetime financial cap** on total return from the initiative. Above the cap, all value flows to mission work.
- **No unlimited equity upside.** This is not a Silicon-Valley startup; the founder will not be enriched beyond a documented ceiling.
- **The entity is not for sale.** Categorical commitment in the founding documents — no acquisition, no merger, no sale at any price. Structurally protects the mission from being redirected by new ownership.

These commitments are enforced in the legal entity's articles of incorporation, not promises. Annual audit verifies compliance. See `FINANCIAL_MODEL.md` for full details, tentative figures, and governance.

### Why this matters

Many open-source projects are extractive: volunteers contribute, the company profits, the founder sells for millions. We commit, structurally, that this is not that model. **The majority of value at scale flows to mission work, not founder wealth.** You can verify this in the published transparency reports, not just trust it.

## How to Contribute (Practical)

### For written contributions

1. Fork the repository.
2. Create a branch with a descriptive name.
3. Make your changes. Keep commits focused and messages clear.
4. Open a pull request with a clear description of what you changed and why.
5. Engage with review feedback openly. Disagreement is welcome; obstruction is not.

### For proposals that change principles, strategy, or governance

1. Open an issue, not a pull request, first.
2. State clearly what you propose to change and why.
3. Allow time for discussion (at least two weeks for significant changes).
4. If consensus emerges, a maintainer will invite a pull request.
5. If consensus does not emerge, the proposal rests. You are free to fork.

### For reporting concerns, harassment, or safety issues

Follow the escalation process in `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md` section 6.

## Style and Quality

### Writing

- Clarity over cleverness.
- Short sentences where possible.
- Cite sources. Use permalinks when citing web content.
- Quran citations: `Surah Name X:Y` format.
- Hadith citations: `Collection, Book, Number` format with grading when available.
- Historical claims require sources.

### Code (when we get there)

- Readable over clever.
- Tested where testability is meaningful.
- Documented such that a new contributor can understand it in 6 months.
- Accessible — assume users with disabilities.
- Internationalizable — assume RTL scripts, non-Latin characters.

## Attribution

- You retain credit for your contributions.
- Commits are signed under your chosen identity (real name or durable pseudonym).
- Co-contributors are acknowledged.
- Plagiarism is grounds for removal.

---

## Pseudonymous Contribution

Some contributors may face safety risks from contributing openly under their real name — especially those based in jurisdictions where this work could be politically or legally dangerous. Pseudonymous contribution is welcomed, provided:

- The pseudonym is durable (you use it consistently).
- The pseudonym does not impersonate a real person.
- Other contributors respect the pseudonym and do not attempt to unmask.

Safety matters more than bureaucratic formality.

---

## Disagreement

You will disagree with decisions. That is healthy. When you do:

1. State your disagreement in the open, clearly.
2. Listen to counter-arguments in good faith.
3. Accept that you may lose the argument.
4. If the decision violates a core principle, escalate through governance.
5. If the project abandons its principles, fork. This is a right, not a betrayal.

---

## When Contributions are Refused

Contributions may be refused for:

- Violating principles or code of conduct.
- Poor quality that cannot be improved through review.
- Scope mismatch (right idea, wrong project).
- Safety concerns (introduces risks to users).
- Sectarian bias.

Refusal is always accompanied by a reason. You may revise and resubmit unless the refusal was for principled reasons that will not change.

## A Final Invitation

If you are a Muslim developer, engineer, researcher, scholar, or student reading this — know that you are needed. Not because of some grand destiny, but because work like this is built by ordinary people over long periods of time. The Ummah's capacity in this domain is built one contribution at a time, by people like you.

If you are a non-Muslim ally reading this — know that you are welcomed. The Prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him, collaborated with non-Muslims throughout his life. The Constitution of Medina is a foundational document of Muslim political thought precisely because it formalized partnership across religious lines. We honor that tradition.

Begin wherever you can. The smallest contribution in the right direction matters more than the grandest plan never executed.

*Bismillah.*
